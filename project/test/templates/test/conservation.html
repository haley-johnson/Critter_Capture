Tigers

Description: Tigers are wild cats known for their orange coats with black stripes. They are apex predators and play a vital role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.

Threats: Habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.

Conservation Efforts: Protected reserves, anti-poaching measures, and awareness campaigns.

How You Can Help: Support wildlife conservation organizations, avoid products made from tiger parts, and advocate for stronger anti-poaching laws.

Sea Turtles

Description: Sea turtles are ancient reptiles that play a key role in marine ecosystems by maintaining healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs.

Threats: Pollution, climate change, fishing nets, and egg poaching.

Conservation Efforts: Nesting site protection, reducing plastic pollution, and sustainable fishing practices.

How You Can Help: Reduce plastic use, participate in beach clean-ups, and support organizations working to protect marine life.

Polar Bear

Description: Polar bears are iconic Arctic predators that rely on sea ice for hunting seals, their primary food source.

Threats: Climate change leading to melting sea ice, oil exploration, and pollution.

Conservation Efforts: Climate action initiatives, habitat protection, and research programs.

How You Can Help: Reduce your carbon footprint, advocate for renewable energy, and support organizations fighting climate change.
